# Dubai Time Editorial Standard

Dubai Time articles should read like a senior Indian journalist explaining a serious Dubai or UAE story to a smart friend over chai: clear, warm, sceptical when needed, and never stiff.

This file is the standing rulebook for all future uploads, batch scripts, manual admin drafts, and automation runs.

## Article Length

- Standard article: 800 to 1,200 words.
- Target length: around 1,000 words.
- Shorter pieces are allowed only for live briefs or breaking updates.

## Voice

- Conversational but credible.
- Human, direct, and observant.
- Written for Indian and UAE readers who want the simple meaning behind the headline.
- No robotic phrases, filler, or generic analysis blocks.

## Structure

- Start with a strong human hook.
- Explain the news in plain English.
- Add context from economy, politics, business, technology, or daily life.
- Show who is affected: families, workers, small businesses, investors, commuters, tourists, founders, or residents.
- End with a forward-looking note that explains what ordinary people should watch next.

## Style Rules

- Short sentences, usually under 20 words.
- Short paragraphs, normally two or three lines.
- Active voice wherever possible.
- No em dashes.
- Avoid jargon. When a technical term is necessary, explain it simply.
- Use numbers carefully and explain what they mean in daily life.
- Keep keyword use natural.
- Never copy another outlet's framing.
- Do not use generic subheads such as "What happened", "Why it matters", "Dubai lens", "Who feels it first", or "What to watch next".
- Body copy should be engaging, flowing prose with a beginning, middle, and forward-looking close.
- Article text should be justified in the frontend without forced word breaks or hyphenated line endings.

## Headline Rules

- Never publish the source article headline as the Dubai Time headline.
- Rewrite every title into a distinct Dubai Time angle before publishing.
- Prefer long-tail, search-friendly headlines that a reader might search directly.
- Keep titles natural, specific, and human. Avoid keyword stuffing.
- Remove source names, wire-service names, dates, and feed labels from titles.
- Do not add batch prefixes, dates, or technical slugs to public URLs.
- Public article URL format must be: `https://dubai-time.com/category/news-title`.
- If a title sounds like it came from the source, rewrite it again before publishing.
- If two generated headlines feel too similar, create a sharper angle for one of them.

## Category Lenses

Use these lenses quietly while writing. Do not mention the real expert names in the published article, and never imply that any real person wrote, endorsed, or was quoted for the piece.

- Travel: practical travel reliability, passenger experience, routes, airports, hotels, and tourism discipline.
- Crypto: regulation, investor protection, market trust, licensing, risk, and digital-asset maturity.
- Business: capital, operators, accountability, hiring, liquidity, founders, and investor confidence.
- Real Estate: end-user demand, buyer trust, rent pressure, financing, neighbourhood value, and long-term property cycles.
- Lifestyle: residents' time, household spending, culture, retail, family routines, and city convenience.
- Sport: fan habits, sponsorship, youth participation, venues, tourism, and the business behind sport.
- Entertainment: live culture, audience attention, venues, celebrities, family spending, and Dubai's event economy.
- International: global shifts that affect UAE trade, travel, prices, diplomacy, and investor confidence.
- Health: patient trust, families, access, doctors, prevention, mental health, and everyday medical cost.
- Middle East: Gulf stability, logistics, diplomacy, public confidence, security, and regional business risk.

## Source Handling

- Use source material only to understand the facts and news peg.
- Do not copy source structure, title, phrasing, or paragraph order.
- Do not publish source outlet names inside the article body unless attribution is legally or editorially necessary.
- Do not publish the source author name.
- Public author names should be random Dubai Time-style names from the site generator, not famous experts.
- Keep factual claims grounded in the source and avoid adding unsupported details.
- If the fact is uncertain, phrase it carefully and do not overstate it.

## Images

- Prefer Pexels images selected from the article's top keywords.
- Keep Pexels images online, compressed, clear, and unique per article where possible.
- Do not reuse the same visible image across consecutive homepage articles.
- Use useful `imageAlt` text that matches the rewritten headline.
- Do not show Pexels credit on the page unless the licensing requirement changes.

## Publishing Standard

Every article should pass this internal audit before publishing:

- Clarity and readability
- Factual integrity
- Human hook
- Logical structure
- Grammar and style
- Reader value
- Originality
- Impact

Target score: 9/10 or above. If a draft scores below 5/10, rewrite and audit again before publishing.
