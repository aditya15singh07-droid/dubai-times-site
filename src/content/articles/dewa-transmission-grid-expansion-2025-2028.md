Dubai Electricity and Water Authority commissioned eight 132kV substations and two 400kV substations in 2025 as part of its plan to strengthen Dubai's electricity transmission network.

DEWA said electricity transmission projects currently under construction exceed AED8.5 billion. The pipeline includes 52 new 132kV substations, 223 kilometres of 132kV underground transmission cables, two 400kV substations and 130 kilometres of 400kV overhead transmission lines scheduled for completion by 2028.

By the end of 2025, DEWA's network across Dubai had reached 394 substations, including 27 at 400kV and 367 at 132kV.

## Grid Buildout Behind The Boom

Dubai's electricity network is being expanded at the same time the city is adding new communities, tourism districts, industrial capacity and digital infrastructure. That timing is important. Fast-growing cities can lose momentum when utilities lag behind construction, but Dubai is trying to keep the power network ahead of demand rather than chasing it after bottlenecks appear.

The scale of the planned substations also points to confidence in long-term load growth. New homes, hotels, offices, data services, logistics hubs and manufacturing facilities all need dependable transmission capacity before they can become fully productive assets.

## Where The Capacity Will Be Felt

- Developers need grid readiness before large communities can be handed over smoothly.
- Industrial and logistics operators need reliable power for production, cold storage, warehousing and automation.
- Hospitality operators depend on stable energy for cooling, guest services and peak-season demand.
- Residents benefit when utility planning reduces the risk of pressure on services in fast-growing districts.

## Investor Read

The business story is execution capacity. Dubai's growth targets depend on the systems behind the skyline: power, water, mobility, cooling, data and logistics. When those systems scale ahead of demand, confidence becomes easier for developers, employers and investors to price in.

For companies looking at Dubai, grid reliability is not a background detail. It affects offices, industrial zones, data-heavy services, hospitality assets and future residential communities.

The next signal to watch is delivery discipline. If DEWA's 2028 project pipeline lands on schedule, it will quietly support everything from new communities to energy-intensive digital infrastructure.

## Next Signal To Track

The most important marker will be how the 2028 transmission pipeline aligns with new residential and commercial handovers. If power infrastructure is delivered in step with construction, Dubai can protect its reputation for reliability while adding density across new growth corridors.
